    Our Programs

    Academic Program

    Curriculum is an important part of any educational system. In Little Hearts Learning Centre we follow a functional curriculum for academics viz English, Math and Science. These subjects are taught through concrete activities which enhance their understanding. However, the goals are set according to the profile of the student and the methodology is based on the learning style of the student. We also use computers to teach the subjects.

    We offer Pratham and NIOS (10th & 12th) for students eligible for the same.

    Applied Behaviour Analysis (ABA)

    It is a highly structured, scientific approach that teaches play, communication, self care, academic and social living skills, and reduces problematic behaviors. A therapist through repetition, reinforcement, and encouragement, helps the child learn skills.

    Sensory Integration & Occupational Therapy

    Our Creative Occupational Therapy team helps children achieve independence in various aspects of their daily lives. We facilitate play, learning and self care skills by improving their fine motor skills, sensory processing abilities, behavioral and visual perceptual skills in a fun and engaging setting.

    Speech Therapy

    Therapists work with children having speech related disorders. We provide screening, assessment and programs related to the needs of the child.

    Spider and Movement Therapy

    Our dedicated therapists work to acquire maximum functional mobility in children with motor disability. We use specialized movement techniques to address gross motor deficit in walking, balance, strength, range of movement and safety in a playful and interactive environment.

    Vocational Training

    Providing vocational training for persons with disabilities is very important as it will enable them to acquire skills that are relevant to current labor demands. Employment is considered a vital step towards their rehabilitation and empowerment as it provides a sense of belonging, independence and increases social inclusion and overall well being of people with disabilities and their families.
